# Codex Goal and Visual Contract

Your goal is to build a complete, carefully authored, procedural ASCILINE character based on the supplied design board and the visual specification in `assets/reference/README.md`.

The finished character must be a deterministic, programmatically constructed wizard that can:

- stand naturally in a fixed white environment
- idle and blink
- speak and move its mouth
- display multiple expressions
- turn toward eight directions
- walk left and right
- walk toward and away from the camera
- walk diagonally
- follow arbitrary paths
- walk clockwise and counterclockwise in circles
- stop, turn, and resume without visual jumps
- point, explain, think, react, and cast magic
- move while speaking
- stream live through ASCILINE
- render without a normal video element
- remain stable during long-running broadcasts

This must be built procedurally from cell masks, vector-like cell primitives, joint positions, palette values, and animation functions.

Do not use generated animation frames, prerecorded video, a pre-rendered runtime sprite sheet, or a flattened character PNG.

The runtime character must be constructed from editable, versioned data and deterministic rendering code.

The character is:

- an elderly male wizard
- long white and gray beard
- blue wizard hat
- yellow or gold stars on the hat
- wide gold hat band
- blue robe
- gold robe trim
- magenta inner robe
- gold belt and buckle
- brown wooden staff
- curled staff head
- small cyan magical orb or gem
- warm tan face
- large readable eyebrows
- slightly witty and intelligent expression
- no wings

Do not add rainbow wings or reuse earlier winged-wizard concepts.

The character should look like a deliberate colored-ASCII character, not a Minecraft render or a smooth modern illustration.
