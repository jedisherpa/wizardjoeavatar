- Tall bright-gold crenellated block crown with a continuous square band, alternating high posts, and visible brown hair inside and below the crown.
- Light warm skin; rectangular green eyes with white sclera; thick dark-brown horizontal brows; long block nose; thick dark-brown mustache; squared jaw and ears.
- Bright-gold knee-length open jacket with squared shoulders and long sleeves; plain white shirt; brown belt with a square gold buckle; tan trousers; dark-brown block boots.

Brows, eyelids, eyes, mouth/jaw, neck, shoulders, elbows, wrists, block hands, hips, knees, ankles, and the whole-character root may articulate. The coat may follow the torso and legs with limited secondary overlap. Crown, skull, mustache, costume construction, prop dimensions, apparent age, and voxel scale do not deform.

## Performance identity

Thorne is restrained, strategic, and authoritative without theatrical tyranny. His motion uses squared posture, deliberate gaze changes, economical sword arcs, measured parchment presentation, decisive pauses, and crisp recoveries. Approval is guarded; disapproval is firm rather than explosive. He never brandishes the sword recklessly and never uses smoking as a character beat.

## Stable production framing

- Every runtime-source panel is a complete crown-to-boot full body.
- A common root and boot baseline must be established during deterministic cell conversion; translation is applied at the root rather than baked into inconsistent panel framing.
- At least a four-cell transparent safety inset is required after panel extraction and cell reconstruction, expanded where the sword, reaching hand, jump, or fall creates the widest silhouette.
