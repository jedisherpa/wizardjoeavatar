# Draven Holt — Identity Lock

## Recognition summary

Draven Holt is a tall, lean, medium-brown adult male operations lead rendered as a polished three-dimensional voxel sculpture. His instant-recognition cluster is the long right-swept black block forelock, heavy square black glasses around luminous green square eyes, full squared black beard and mustache, black-on-black suit, and one vivid orange vertical tie. His approved work kit is a brown clipboard with silver clip and an orange mechanical pencil. He is disciplined and decisive rather than aggressive.

## Immutable identity features

### Silhouette and proportions

- Tall, lean adult silhouette with long straight trouser legs and moderately broad squared shoulders.
- Head is a large voxel block but remains below one third of standing height.
- Torso is straight and tailored; limbs use the same small-cube construction as the head.
- Black block shoes have short stepped toes and a stable flat baseline.
- Cube size, head-to-body ratio, shoulder width, limb length, hand size, and shoe size must not change between poses.

### Head and face

- Medium-brown rectangular face and ears.
- Asymmetrical swept black block hair: raised ridges at crown, a pronounced forelock projecting toward the character's right, and a rounded stepped back mass.
- Thick square black glasses with a strong bridge and deep rims.
- Large luminous emerald-green square irises with dark square pupils, aligned inside the glasses.
- Full squared black beard wrapping jaw and chin, joined to a straight block mustache.
- Small projecting block nose; restrained mouth aperture remains inside the beard/mustache construction.
- Apparent age, skull proportions, eye spacing, glasses dimensions, hair direction, and beard outline are invariant.

### Costume

- Matte near-black tailored voxel suit jacket with visible lapel structure.
- Black shirt, black straight trousers, black stepped block shoes.
- Vivid orange straight tie centered on the front torso, ending above a small silver-gray belt or tie-end accent.
- No alternate jacket, vest, hat, jewelry, badges, or decorative trim.

### Approved props

- Brown rectangular voxel clipboard with stable thickness and proportions.
- Silver-gray block clip at top center.
- Clean white paper carrying only abstract gray checkbox-and-bar marks; never readable words or numbers.
- Orange mechanical pencil with gray cap/tip and stable length.
- Default presentation: clipboard cradled near the character's left side while the right hand gives concise directives. Transfers may temporarily change the grip but not the prop design.
- A cigar appears in the historical source image but is explicitly removed from the approved character. Cigars, cigarettes, tobacco, smoke, vapor, and smoking gestures are prohibited in every production derivative.

### Rendering lock

- Polished high-quality 3D voxel sculpture made from uniform small cubes.
- Crisp cube edges, subtle ambient occlusion, soft cool studio lighting, and a blue-gray seamless studio environment.
- Stable orthographic-reference camera feel and consistent camera distance within each production sheet.
- No outlines, painted pixels, flat sprite look, photoreal skin, clay smoothing, or mixed rendering medium.

## Features allowed to articulate

- Eyelids, brow/eye aperture, and pupil emphasis may change for expressions and blinks while glasses and eye placement stay fixed.
- Mouth aperture may form the approved visemes inside the fixed beard/mustache opening.
- Head, neck, shoulders, spine, elbows, wrists, hips, knees, ankles, hands, and simplified fingers may articulate without changing segment length or cube scale.
- Jacket panels and orange tie may follow motion subtly but may not change construction or detach.
- Clipboard and pencil may rotate, transfer, be offered, released, or received during approved action arcs.
- Posture may open, compress, lean, turn, crouch, jump, fall, and recover while preserving root logic and body proportions.

## Performance lock

- Default posture is squared, upright, attentive, and grounded.
- Movement is concise: short anticipation, clear primary action, economical follow-through, immediate clean recovery.
- Directives use short open-hand gestures or one confirming point.
- Listening lowers gesture amplitude and settles the hands without appearing passive.
- Emotion is legible but controlled. Ownership and reliability must never be mistaken for hostility.

## Never invent or change

- No smoking object or effect.
- No weapons, phone, mug, book, badge, jewelry, hat, alternate eyewear, or alternate work prop.
- No different hair direction, beard cut, glasses shape, eye color, skin color, tie color, costume seam layout, or apparent age.
- No extra or missing limbs, malformed hands, floating anatomy, or changed cube density.
- No readable text, logo, number, watermark, or caption inside a production cell.

## Canonical recognition test

Every pose must remain recognizable at silhouette scale from the swept hair, square glasses, beard block, tailored black suit, and orange tie. When the face is visible, the luminous green square eyes must remain centered behind the same glasses. When a work prop is present, it must match the approved clipboard or orange pencil exactly.
