## Character analysis

- **Silhouette:** tall, slender robed angel; broad symmetrical stepped wings span approximately twice the shoulder width; circular halo rises completely above the head; robe widens toward a grounded hem.
- **Proportions:** approximately 6.5 heads tall including the halo, with a compact head, narrow shoulders, long robe, and small block feet. Wings begin behind the shoulders and extend up and outward.
- **Face:** softly squared voxel face; straight dark-brown rectangular eyes placed symmetrically; small projecting block nose; tiny neutral mouth; no visible ears, facial hair, or makeup.
- **Hair:** bright orange stepped bob with a rounded block fringe and longer side locks. Hair must not become blonde, brown, long, loose, or differently parted.
- **Headwear:** a complete circular golden-orange halo, centered behind and above the head, always fully visible.
- **Clothing:** uninterrupted warm-cream floor-length robe with broad cuffed sleeves, modest rounded neckline, vertical block folds, flared stepped hem, and cream block footwear. No belt, jewelry, armor, or extra layer is visible.
- **Wings:** two broad warm-cream voxel-feather wings with stable stepped construction and matching scale; both wing tips must remain inside every panel.
- **Hands and prop:** simplified cream block hands; canonical action cradles one warm glowing orange orb at the sternum. The orb is the only approved prop.
- **Shape language:** stepped voxel curves, broad ascending wing diagonals, circular halo, vertical robe column, and a centered circular orb.
- **Palette:** orange hair `#F29A13`–`#FFB51F`; gold halo/orb `#F6A713`–`#FFD75A`; warm cream robe/wings `#EBCF91`–`#FFF0BD`; warm peach skin `#EFB776`–`#FFD39A`; dark-brown eyes `#4A270F`; pale-blue studio `#9DD8F4`–`#E8F7FF`.
- **Materials:** matte voxel blocks with restrained soft highlights; halo and orb add warm emissive light without washing out cube edges.
- **Rendering:** square-cell voxel geometry, visible cube seams, clean studio perspective, soft frontal-upper lighting, gentle contact shadow, pale-blue background, no outlines, no painterly texture.
- **Default posture:** upright, centered, calm, feet grounded, shoulders lowered, wings open but relaxed, orb held with both hands.
- **Personality in motion:** compassionate, bright, consent-aware, emotionally attentive; slow wing breathing, gentle head inclines, patient pauses, small reassuring gestures, and restrained luminosity. During distress the wings settle and movement becomes plain and still.

## 1. Immutable identity features

1. Orange stepped bob and side locks.
2. Complete centered golden circular halo.
3. Two broad symmetrical warm-cream stepped wings with unchanged construction and span.
4. Tall warm-cream floor-length robe with broad sleeves, vertical folds, flared hem, and small cream footwear.
5. Softly squared face with dark rectangular eyes, small block nose, and tiny mouth.
6. Fixed voxel scale, body proportions, palette, cube seams, studio lighting logic, and material response.
7. One warm glowing orange orb, when a prop is present, at a consistent scale.
8. Complete silhouette, halo, both wing tips, hands, robe hem, and feet inside the canvas safety margin.

## 2. Features allowed to articulate or deform

- Eyelids, eyebrows implied by brow blocks, mouth shapes, cheeks, and head inclination may articulate while preserving the skull and eye design.
- Shoulders, elbows, wrists, fingers/hand blocks, spine, hips, knees, ankles, and robe folds may move within the same block anatomy.
- Wings may breathe, settle, protectively fold, or give restrained beats while preserving root placement, span, feather-step construction, and left/right symmetry.
- Halo may follow the head with slight secondary lag but must remain circular, centered, and fully visible.
- Orb brightness may pulse subtly; size, hue family, and block construction remain fixed.
- Robe folds and hem may compress or trail for locomotion without changing costume construction.

## 3. Hidden details requiring careful extrapolation

- Back hair and rear neckline are not shown directly; use the least-disruptive continuation of the visible bob and plain robe.
- Wing roots and back attachment are obscured; attach symmetrically behind the upper shoulder blades with no harness.
- The back of the robe is unseen; keep it plain cream with the same vertical folds and flared hem.
- Feet and hands are partly obscured; use the canonical simplified block anatomy and never add realistic fingers or footwear ornament.
- The halo's depth is unclear; use one consistent shallow block-ring thickness.

## 4. Details that must not be invented

- No weapon, book, staff, microphone, jewelry, belt, cape, armor, crown, extra wings, tail, horns, or modern accessory.
- No printed symbols, text, insignia, feathers detached from the voxel construction, or decorative robe trim.
- No new facial features, eye colors, hairstyle changes, age changes, or costume color changes.
- No asymmetric wing damage or accessory placement without explicit approval.

## 5. Multiple-interpretation areas

- The orb may be magical light or a symbolic mentoring object; production treats it only as a stable luminous prop, not a creature or weapon.
- Wing feather count is stylized rather than anatomical; the canonical stepped silhouette, not a literal feather count, is authoritative.
- The original background suggests a sunset setting, while the canonical derivative establishes the approved pale-blue studio presentation. Worksheets use the canonical studio.

## Runtime framing lock

Runtime-oriented panels use a complete full-body silhouette at a stable camera distance and baseline. At least ten percent clear space must remain around the widest wing/halo/extremity silhouette. Bust framing is forbidden for expression, viseme, locomotion, signature-action, and interaction boards.
