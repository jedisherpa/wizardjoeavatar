## Immutable identity features

- Adult feminine bright-lime-green alien with a very large rectangular cuboid head and a slim, upright body.
- Enormous black stepped almond eyes with outward feminine lash blocks, two tiny square black nostrils, and a small horizontal black mouth. Do not humanize the face or add hair, ears, eyebrows, or a human nose.
- Tailored burnt-orange single-breasted jacket, matching straight knee-length skirt, cream V-neck blouse, slim green legs, and orange block heels.
- Simplified green voxel hands with the same construction and scale in every pose.
- Silver cuboid microphone head, black stem, and green hand grip when the microphone is present.
- High-detail small-cube voxel construction, pale cool-blue studio, faint tiled floor, soft cool key light, restrained contact shadow, and stable cube scale.

Species, apparent age, head dimensions, eye/lash construction, face placement, body proportions, clothing construction, palette, heel shape, microphone scale, and cube size must not change between panels.

## Features allowed to articulate

Eyelids, pupils/eye aperture, mouth, neck, shoulders, elbows, wrists, hands, fingers, hips, knees, ankles, jacket hem, and skirt may articulate. The rectangular skull may tilt and rotate but must not squash, taper, round, or change size. The jacket and skirt may follow the body without changing cut or length.

## Performance lock

Elara is warm, unhurried, intelligent, and exacting. She uses measured presenting arcs, deliberate microphone handoffs, precise sequencing gestures, small reflective head tilts, attentive listening stillness, and calm corrective emphasis. Serious or protective states reduce flourish, lower the microphone, close the stance, and slow the recovery.

## Production rejection rules

Reject any panel that humanizes Elara, adds hair or ears, changes the rectangular head, alters the stepped eye/lash design, changes wardrobe colors or construction, removes or rescales the heels, invents props, changes digit/limb count, crops any silhouette or microphone, uses bust framing on a runtime sheet, changes the voxel scale or studio, or places a grounded foot off the shared baseline without an intentional airborne action.
