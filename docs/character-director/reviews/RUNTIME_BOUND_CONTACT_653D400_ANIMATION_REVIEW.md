   directional push. The final stop at 192-193 is comparatively clean.
3. **Turns lose silhouette and staff continuity.** The character snaps to full back
   at frame 214 while the staff swaps screen sides. The return holds back-facing,
   jumps through profile at 235, then reaches front at 236. Add authored turn
   intermediates that preserve hand/staff ownership and a readable rotation arc.
4. **Speech articulation is binary.** The mouth opens around frame 285, holds nearly
   unchanged through frame 317, and closes by frame 324. Add syllabic mouth timing,
   active speaking/listening posture, and a visible body reaction to interruption.
